Who am I?

My name is Juan Diego Castano Luna, and I’m a very proud Canadian-Colombian. I came to Canada when I was two year old and haven't looked back because the opportunities provided here have simply been amazing. Cadets has been a great opportunity, I’ve been a cadet since I was nine years old, and just recently started my paperwork to join the Royal Canadian Reserves. I've also been in the workforce since grade ten, working downtown as a Water Taxi driver, and yes, I have all my licences. I’ve always been interested in programming because of the endless number of things that can be done with it -- being able to create something that you thought up in your head and making it a reality has always fascinated me. This is one of the reasons I have been inclined to engineering, design and programming. These three pillars are something I want to integrate into my future career and are things that will hopefully make me an indispensable member of a team one day.

Here's a little back story starting with grade ten. I started working with Arduinos in grade ten, and integrating it with 3D printed designs I created at home with Autocad and Solidworks. Creating a program, integrating the hardware and then designing the part required a lot of problem solving skills and a lot of late nights, however, the things I learnt have been priceless. I am a strong believer of learning from your mistakes and this is what these projects provided me with a place to explore my capabilities and a place to expand my knowledge. Another area of interest I have is finance, how can someone practically create a thing that solves a specific issue without having good financial literacy? I have begun investing in stocks - and yes, of course - ethereum which is a very advanced decentralized currency that integrates decentralized applications -- a topic of great interest to me. Programming is a very important pillar to ethereum and fintech, and if you havent noticed its a very important part of my life.
